NEUROLOGIX PRO: CLINICAL-GRADE MRI DIAGNOSTIC SYNTHESIS
=====================================================

PROJECT OBJECTIVE:
Automated multi-class classification and spatial localization of brain tumors 
from MRI scans to support clinical decision-making.

1. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE
-----------------------------------------------------
*   Primary Classifier: EfficientNetB4 (Baseline) and Hybrid Swin-Transformer.
*   Detection Module: YOLOv8 integrated for rapid ROI (Region of Interest) tracking.
*   Segmentation Module: U-Net architecture for pixel-level mass delineation.
*   Explainability Engine: Grad-CAM++ (Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping).
    - Uses 1st, 2nd, and 3rd order gradients.
    - Provides high-precision localization compared to standard Grad-CAM.
*   Backend: Flask-based clinical workbench.

2. METHODOLOGY & PREPROCESSING
-----------------------------------------------------
The pipeline implements specific medical imaging standards:
*   Brain Contour Extraction: Gaussian Blur + Thresholding + Contour detection 
    to isolate brain tissue from skull/background.
*   CLAHE (Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization): Normalizes image 
    contrast for better feature detection in low-contrast MRI slices.
*   Z-Score Normalization: Standardizes intensity distribution based on 
    dataset-wide mean and standard deviation.
*   Image Resizing: Normalized to 224x224 pixels (optimized for Transformer/CNN hybrids).

3. DATASET & CLASSIFICATION TARGETS
-----------------------------------------------------
The model is trained to identify and localize four distinct categories:
1.  Glioma: Primary brain tumor starting in glial cells.
2.  Meningioma: Tumor arising from the meninges (membranes around the brain).
3.  Pituitary: Tumor located in the pituitary gland.
4.  No Tumor: Healthy healthy tissue calibration.

4. TRAINING STRATEGY
-----------------------------------------------------
*   Optimizer: AdamW (Adam with Weight Decay) for stable convergence.
*   Loss Function: Cross-Entropy Loss with focal weights.
*   Scheduling: ReduceLROnPlateau (factor=0.5, patience=3).
*   Imbalance Handling: WeightedRandomSampler to prevent bias toward healthy scans.
*   Hardware Acceleration: NVIDIA Mixed Precision Training (AMP) for reduced latency.

5. CLINICAL WORKBENCH (FRONT-END)
-----------------------------------------------------
*   Side-by-Step Visualization: Original scan vs. Grad-CAM++ heatmap.
*   Diagnostic Breakdown: Percentage-based confidence score per class.
*   ROI Delineation: Automated bounding boxes around detected anomalies 
    based on activation peaks.

6. KEY RESEARCH INNOVATION
-----------------------------------------------------
The primary research value of this project lies in the "Diagnostic Synthesis" 
approach—combining high-accuracy classification with Grad-CAM++ localization. 
This ensures the model is not a "black box" but provides radiological proof 
for its findings by highlighting the exact spatial regions influencing 
the classification.
